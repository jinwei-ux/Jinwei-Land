DETAILED DESIGN
We used all the WMC theme color and fonts for detailed design, and we designed all the icons or shapes that were used in this project.
We made hi-fi prototype for better presenting our design. Check online prototype by click this link.
The most challenging task in this design was to finish the development of the mobile version without extra working hours, because of the launch date was getting close. It was definitely a nightmare for the frontend engineers.

From our side, we must design a development friendly interface to save time and also take care of a coherent user experience. Through the discussion with the development lead and design team, we finally changed the way we put the menu button in uploading page, from side-bar type to top-bar type. In this way we believe it would save at least 80% of the development cost.
The compatibility among different browsers and systems was another huge problem to solve. We designed a contactless experience of taking facial recognition photo, to make this step less privacy concerned. At last we ditched some browsers, like IE, and mainly focused on the popular ones, to balance the cost and the pay.

We also changed the font style and the whole layout of the interface, and the final result of both desktop and mobile had great coherency and user experience.

Apart from widget, we also gave support to design the onsite check-in machine. It was an Android device, running Sensetime SDK for facial recognition.
The interface on this device was rather simple and indicative. When the attendee came close, it showed the result of facial recognition, green for pass, orange for stop. If the attendees previously got themselves registered successfully from the widget, they would have green. If no match detected from the database, a orange sign would show. Also a still picture wouldn't be accepted as a live face. While multi-match happened, attendees will be guided to a manual ID check.

A Magical Widget - User Guide
Through the entrance in the personal information session, we designed a pop-up window to guide the users complete uploading photo. Users needed to confirm two photos for joining the event, facial recognition photo and profile photo, separately for access control use and networking use.
The workflow included two individual parts. When uploading photos, users could choose either from camera or from local files. The photo for facial recognition had extra step for safety concern, which was that users needed to validate that the photo could be recognized as themselves when they check in on-site.

We made a connection between facial recognition photo and profile photo. For those users without former profile photo, we provided an option that they could directly use facial recognition photo as profile photo.
When users upload photo from local file, the photo they uploaded would be validated to make sure the quality was good enough for the safe use. We made retristriction for local files, they must be no larger than 5 mb in color and also minimum as 600*600 px.

The quality verification process would give feedback to users when they failed to upload a qualified photo. The situations for a failed attempt could be:

1. Multiple faces were detected;
2. No face was detected;
3. Face size in the photo was too small or too big;
4. Face occlusion was detected;
5. Face was too far away from the center of photo;
6. Part of facial feature was missing;
7. Mouth open too big;

Each case had a specific feedback, helping users upload a qualified photo. After a qualified facial recognition photo was taken or uploaded, users had 4 attempts to validate the photo by comparing the photo with live stream. If they ran out all the attempts, they could choose to try again or reupload the facial recognition photo. Each step was designed to make the check-in process safe and accurate.
We made lo-fi prototype to try our design among the company and collaborators. They are from different business unit and all the potential users.

When we put the taking biometric feature step after the personal information step, users found it a little unexpected and some of them said “I thought the personal information was completed”. Result showed that in users’ mind model, biometric feature belongs to personal information, though it was of great privacy concern, it still was part of the personal information.  
At last, we put the entrance in the personal information table, and attendees could take the option to upload photo for facial feature taking. The traditional way of check-in was still an option. If attendees felt uncomfortable with biometric identification method as access control, they can still go on-site and use badge to access the event.
We used the power of copywriting to ease the privacy concerns of the users. The highlight of benefits of biometric recognition method ensured the users that they no longer had to wait for long time when check-in. When a specific use case was given, it was proved by the questionnaire that the acceptance rate could be higher than that without a use case shown to the users. And the benefits would be a plus to encourage users to use new method for access control.

There are two steps to make the magic happen:
1. Users leave their facial feature on the registration process;
2. Users go on-site and enter the access control;

Step 1 is very privacy sensitive and we need to ease the doubtness and anxiety by designing a safe entrance and building a trustworthy environment.

Different users based around the globe have very distinguishing perspectives about personal privacy online. Among all the users, around 48% of the sample group thought it SAFE to leave biological information for identification use in a business meeting or paid exhibition.
People hate waiting. We need to shorten the waiting time. For check-in, users commonly feel comfortable when it takes less than 2 minutes, while 15 minutes reaches the bottom line and makes 89% users feel angry or impatient.
For identification use in the WMC summit, 73% of the users are willing to provide facial feature information to cut the waiting time from 3 minutes to 5 seconds. It’s an interesting fact that the acceptance rate was much higher than posting personal information online.
What Customers Say, How Do They Feel
THE DISCOVERY
We conducted customer and market research to validate our approach proposal. Online questionnaire was used to do the user research. These are the key insights that help shaping the final product.
According to the research, we found out that the situation about privacy concerns varied from region to region. The users in North America and Australia were found to be the most sensitive group regarding posting personal profile picture online, while people in Asia were less worried about personal information on the internet.
Simplicity Privacy & Revolution
THE APPORACH
Facing a more severe situation, the manual check-in process was hard to meet the requirements, even took a great deal of time and money. We redesigned the check-in process with high efficient automatic check-in method, utilising facial recognition technology and camera embedded check-in kiosk, to boost the whole check-in process.
Comparing to the traditional check-in methods, the redesigned solution simplified the procedure and turned manual check-in to an automatic way by facial recognition.

According to the field research we conducted, manual check-in on average takes 90 seconds to complete the process, including passport scanning, information checking, badge printing, and etc. While automatic check-in method only takes up to 10 seconds, with 1 sec facial recognition and the rest for passing by. It could save up to 80% customer’s waiting time, as well as the cost of counter staff.

In order to make the revolution happen, we need to add one extra step during the registration process, which is to collect the facial feature. It indeed took extra time and made the registration process more complicated than it already was. But we believed the magic check-in experience would finally make it all worthwhile.

Apart from the refined online registration system, there was another problem that aroused even more attention. The privacy concern would strike an alarm when customers register themselves online.

With the publish of GDPR laws in Europe, the private information used online became more sensitive. We were tasked to design a less personal information sensitive interaction for customers from around the world.
The old way of check-in was done completely manually by the counter staff. It was time consuming and needed a lot of human resources. We used to work closely with event management company Aventri, with more than 40 lanes in each hall open for attendants. However, the increasing number of attendants made the check-in process boring and redundant. Attendants had to wait for long to get checked and entered the exhibition zone after a long queue. That’s the main problem we needed to challenge.

Our challenge was to maximally decrease the check-in waiting time and make the enrollment as fast as possible.
